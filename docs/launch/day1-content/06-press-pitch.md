The pitch in one sentence: the situational awareness that used to require a Bloomberg terminal or a Janes subscription, now open-source and free.

**What it does:**

WorldPulse ingests signals from wire services (AP, Reuters, AFP), government feeds (USGS, NOAA, WHO), and OSINT sources (ACLED, OpenSanctions, GDELT). Each signal passes through an AI-powered pipeline that classifies, geolocates, and cross-references it against related events — producing reliability scores and a verification timeline.

The result is a live interactive world map with conflict, natural hazard, cyber threat, and maritime layers, plus 9 specialized intelligence dashboards covering everything from sanctions tracking to internet outages.

**Why it matters:**

- OSINT analysts currently cobble together a dozen free tools or pay $30K+/year for enterprise platforms
- Most intelligence tools are proprietary — you can't audit how they determine what's "reliable"
- WorldPulse is fully transparent: MIT license, public reliability scores, open knowledge graph
- 55,000+ intelligence signals indexed and growing, with 178 active feeds monitored continuously
